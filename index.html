Inflation rises to a record high

of 17.1 percent

Inflation in the Netherlands rose to 17.1 percent in September.
This is reported by the Central Bureau of Statistics (CBS) on
the basis of the European harmonized consumer price index
(HICP). According to Statistics Netherlands, this is in any case
the highest inflation since the Second World War. In August it
was still 13.7 percent, which was also a record. The increased
energy prices are an important cause: compared to September last
year, energy prices including fuel have increased by 114 percent
this month. Statistics Netherlands includes the prices of new
energy contracts when calculating inflation. For many people,
energy inflation will have been lower, for example because they
still have a permanent contract. And many people with a variable
contract did not experience such a large price increase in
September.
